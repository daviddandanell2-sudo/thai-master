# Content Rules

## Every Page Must Answer Real Parent Questions

Before writing any page, identify the parent question it answers. If you can't name the question, don't write the page.

**Examples of real parent questions:**
- "How do I find a good math tutor in Bangkok?"
- "What is IGCSE and do I need a tutor for it?"
- "My child has ADHD. Can a tutor help?"
- "We're moving to Phuket. What schools should we consider?"
- "Is online tutoring as good as in-person?"

## Mandatory Page Structure

Every service page (location, subject, curriculum) must include:

### 1. Clear Headline (H1)
- Include primary keyword
- Be specific, not vague
- Example: "Private Math Tutoring in Bangkok" not "Welcome to Our Tutoring Services"

### 2. Parent Problem Statement
- Acknowledge why the parent is on this page
- Show empathy and understanding
- 2-4 sentences

### 3. Service Explanation
- What tutoring looks like for this specific page
- How it works
- What the parent can expect
- 3-5 paragraphs

### 4. Who the Service Is For
- Specific parent personas this page serves
- Use bullet points for clarity

### 5. Subjects or Curriculum Covered
- Relevant subjects (for location/curriculum pages)
- Relevant curricula (for location/subject pages)
- Links to related pages

### 6. Location Relevance
- Why this location matters for tutoring
- Local context (schools, expat community, etc.)
- 2-3 paragraphs

### 7. Tutor Matching Explanation
- How tutors are selected for this service
- What qualifications matter
- 2-3 paragraphs

### 8. Trust Section
- Why parents can trust this service
- Specific trust signals (not vague claims)
- 2-3 paragraphs

### 9. FAQ Section
- 5-7 real questions parents ask
- Short, direct answers
- Link to related pages where relevant

### 10. Clear Call to Action
- What the parent should do next
- WhatsApp button or contact form reference
- Low-pressure language

### 11. Internal Links to Related Pages
- Minimum 3 contextual links
- Descriptive anchor text
- Relevant to the page topic

## Section Length Guidelines

| Section | Minimum | Maximum | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Headline | 30 chars | 60 chars | Include primary keyword |
| Meta Description | 120 chars | 160 chars | Include keyword + CTA |
| Introduction | 100 words | 200 words | Hook the parent |
| Parent Problem | 50 words | 100 words | Show empathy |
| Service Explanation | 150 words | 300 words | Be specific |
| Who It's For | 50 words | 100 words | Bullet points preferred |
| Subjects/Curriculum | 100 words | 200 words | Include links |
| Location Relevance | 100 words | 200 words | Local details |
| Tutor Matching | 100 words | 200 words | Process + qualifications |
| Trust Section | 100 words | 200 words | Specific signals |
| FAQ | 5 questions | 7 questions | Short answers (50-100 words each) |
| CTA | 1 sentence | 3 sentences | Clear next step |
| Internal Links | 3 links | 7 links | Contextual, descriptive |

**Total page target:** 800-1500 words for service pages. Blog articles: 1200-2500 words.

## What Makes Content "Thin"

Thin content is content that doesn't help the parent make a decision. Avoid:

- Pages under 300 words
- Generic descriptions that could apply to any city
- Lists of subjects without explanation
- Copy-pasted content with only the city name changed
- Content that doesn't answer a specific parent question
- Content that reads like an advertisement, not a resource

## What Makes Content Strong

Strong content helps the parent understand, decide, and act. Include:

- Specific details about the location, subject, or curriculum
- Real parent concerns addressed directly
- Clear explanation of how tutoring works
- Honest assessment of when tutoring helps most
- Specific next steps (contact, book, learn more)
- Links to related resources

## Blog Article Rules

Every blog article must:
- Answer a specific parent question
- Be based on real search intent (not invented topics)
- Include practical, actionable advice
- Link to at least one service page
- Be 1200-2500 words
- Include a clear CTA
- Not be a sales pitch disguised as an article

## Location Page Specific Rules

Every location page must explain:
- Why this location is relevant for tutoring
- What international schools are in the area
- What curricula are common there
- What subjects are in highest demand
- What makes tutoring in this location specific

**Never create a location page that is just:**
> "We offer tutoring in [City]. Our tutors are qualified. Contact us."

## Subject Page Specific Rules

Every subject page must explain:
- What the subject covers at different levels
- Why parents typically seek tutoring for this subject
- Which curricula include this subject
- Which age groups struggle most
- How tutoring helps specifically for this subject

## Curriculum Page Specific Rules

Every curriculum page must explain:
- What the curriculum is (for parents who don't know)
- The exam or assessment structure
- Why families in Thailand choose it
- Which subjects are covered
- How tutoring supports students in this curriculum

## Content Quality Checklist

Before any page is marked complete:
- [ ] Does it answer a real parent question?
- [ ] Is the primary keyword in the H1?
- [ ] Is the meta description 120-160 characters?
- [ ] Does it have at least 800 words?
- [ ] Does it have 5-7 FAQ items?
- [ ] Does it have at least 3 internal links?
- [ ] Does it have a clear CTA?
- [ ] Is the location/subject/curriculum specific enough?
- [ ] Is it different from similar pages?
- [ ] Does it avoid fake claims?
- [ ] Does it match the brand voice?
