# Conversion Rules

## Conversion Strategy

The website must convert parents into inquiries. Every page is a step toward a conversation, not just an information source.

## Main Conversion Actions

1. **WhatsApp Message** — Primary CTA. Fastest path to conversation.
2. **Contact Form** — For parents who prefer email or detailed inquiries.
3. **Book a Consultation** — For parents who want a scheduled call.
4. **Request Tutor Matching** — For parents ready to start the process.
5. **Ask About Pricing** — For parents comparing options.
6. **Ask About Curriculum Support** — For parents with specific curriculum questions.

## CTA Placement Rules

### Above the Fold
Every service page must have a soft CTA within the first 300 words.
- Example: "Have questions about math tutoring in Bangkok? Message us on WhatsApp."

### After Problem Statement
After acknowledging the parent's problem, offer help.
- Example: "If your child is struggling with IGCSE math, we can help. Contact us to discuss your needs."

### Mid-Page
After the service explanation, reinforce the CTA.
- Example: "Ready to find the right tutor? Let's talk."

### Bottom of Page
Every page ends with a clear, specific CTA.
- Example: "Get started with private tutoring in Bangkok. Message us on WhatsApp or fill out the contact form."

### Sticky Element (Optional)
A persistent WhatsApp button or contact link visible while scrolling.

## CTA Copy Rules

### Do
- Use action verbs: "Message," "Contact," "Ask," "Book," "Get Started"
- Be specific: "Message us about IB tutoring" not "Contact us"
- Keep it low-pressure: "Ask a question — no commitment" not "Buy now"
- Match the page topic: "Find a math tutor in Bangkok" on the Bangkok math page

### Don't
- Use aggressive language: "Buy now," "Don't miss out," "Limited time"
- Use vague language: "Click here," "Learn more" (without context)
- Use fear: "Your child will fall behind without tutoring"
- Use fake urgency: "Only 2 tutors left"

## WhatsApp Integration

### WhatsApp CTA Format
- Button text: "Message us on WhatsApp" or "Ask a question on WhatsApp"
- Pre-filled message (optional): "Hi, I'm interested in tutoring for [subject/location]. Can you help?"
- Display phone number clearly for parents who want to save it

### WhatsApp Response Expectations
- Acknowledge inquiry within 2 hours during business hours
- Ask 2-3 clarifying questions (subject, level, location, goals)
- Offer a brief phone call if the inquiry is complex
- Never pressure. Always helpful.

## Contact Form Fields

### Required Fields
- Parent name
- Email address
- Phone number (for WhatsApp follow-up)
- Location (city or area in Thailand)
- Student age/grade

### Optional Fields
- Subject(s) needed
- Curriculum (IB, IGCSE, A Level, etc.)
- Preferred schedule
- Specific goals or concerns
- How they heard about us

### Form Copy
- Headline: "Get in Touch About Tutoring"
- Subtext: "Tell us what you need. We'll get back to you within 24 hours."
- Button: "Send Inquiry"
- Privacy note: "We never share your information."

## Consultation Booking Flow

1. Parent clicks "Book a Consultation"
2. Short form captures: name, email, phone, preferred time
3. Confirmation message: "We'll contact you within 24 hours to confirm your consultation."
4. Follow-up call or message to schedule
5. 15-20 minute consultation to understand needs
6. Tutor matching proposal sent within 48 hours

## Conversion Copy by Page Type

### Location Pages
> "Looking for a tutor in [City]? Tell us what you need and we'll match you with a qualified tutor in your area."

### Subject Pages
> "Need help with [Subject]? Our tutors explain it clearly and help your child build confidence. Get in touch to learn more."

### Curriculum Pages
> "Navigating [Curriculum]? Our tutors understand the system and can help your child succeed. Ask us how."

### Blog Articles
> "Need personalized help? Our tutors work with families across Thailand. Message us to discuss your needs."

## Conversion Metrics to Track

- WhatsApp click rate by page
- Form submission rate by page
- Consultation booking rate by page
- Conversion rate by page type (location vs. subject vs. curriculum)
- Lead quality by source page
- Time from inquiry to tutor match

## A/B Testing Opportunities

- WhatsApp button text: "Message us" vs. "Ask a question" vs. "Get started"
- CTA placement: above fold only vs. above fold + bottom
- Form length: all fields vs. required only
- Consultation offer: "Free consultation" vs. "Book a call" vs. "Ask us anything"
