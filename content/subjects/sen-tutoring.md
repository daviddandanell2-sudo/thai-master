# SEN Tutoring in Thailand

SEN tutoring provides specialized, patient, and individualized support for students with learning differences. Every child learns differently, and for students with ADHD, dyslexia, autism, or other special educational needs, a standard classroom approach is often not enough. One-to-one SEN tutoring can build confidence, address specific learning barriers, and help your child reach their potential in a supportive environment. Our tutors understand that these students are not less capable — they simply need the right approach, the right pace, and the right support to thrive.

## What SEN Tutoring Covers

### ADHD Support
- Breaking tasks into short, focused segments
- Using movement and variety to maintain engagement
- Building organizational systems and routines
- Managing impulsivity and sustaining attention
- Celebrating small wins to build motivation

### Dyslexia Support
- Multi-sensory phonics instruction
- Structured, cumulative reading approaches
- Spelling strategies that work for dyslexic learners
- Assistive technology and tools
- Building reading confidence through patient practice

### Autism Support
- Clear, explicit instructions and expectations
- Visual supports and structured routines
- Sensory-aware learning environments
- Social communication support
- Building on special interests to engage learning

### General Learning Differences
- Identifying how the student learns best
- Adapting teaching style to the individual
- Breaking complex tasks into manageable steps
- Building self-esteem alongside academic skills
- Communicating with parents about progress and strategies

### Confidence Building
- Creating a safe, pressure-free learning space
- Focusing on strengths as well as areas for development
- Setting achievable, incremental goals
- Celebrating progress, not just outcomes
- Helping students understand that different learning paths are valid
- Rebuilding self-esteem after negative school experiences
- Encouraging self-advocacy and communication about needs

## How SEN Tutors Help

An SEN tutor helps students who learn differently to access education on their own terms. Every child deserves to feel capable and supported, and SEN tutoring is about removing barriers rather than fixing deficits. An SEN tutor helps students:

- Learn in a way that matches their individual needs
- Build confidence that may have been damaged by past struggles
- Develop coping strategies and self-advocacy skills
- Catch up on academic content they may have missed
- Prepare for exams with appropriate accommodations and techniques
- Feel understood and supported in their learning journey
- Recognize their own strengths and unique ways of thinking
- Develop the confidence to participate and succeed in any learning environment

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What age should my child start SEN tutoring?**
Any age from 5 upwards. Early support can prevent confidence issues, but students of any age benefit from SEN-aware tutoring.

**Which curriculum does your SEN tutoring follow?**
We follow your child's curriculum, adapting teaching methods to their specific learning needs and any recommendations from educational psychologists or school support staff.

**Do you offer online SEN tutoring?**
Yes, online SEN tutoring can work well, though some students benefit more from in-person sessions depending on their needs.

**How do I know if my child needs SEN tutoring?**
Signs include a diagnosed learning difference, teacher feedback about struggling in class, loss of confidence, or needing more support than school can provide.

**Can a tutor help if my child doesn't have a formal diagnosis?**
Yes. Many students show signs of learning differences before formal assessment. Tutors can use SEN-aware strategies and recommend next steps.

**How long are SEN tutoring sessions?**
Sessions are typically one hour, though some students benefit from shorter, more frequent sessions to maintain focus and engagement.

**What should my child bring to an SEN tutoring session?**
School reports, any assessment documents, current textbooks and assignments, a notebook, and any assistive technology they use.
