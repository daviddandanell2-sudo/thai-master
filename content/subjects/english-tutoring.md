## What English Tutoring Covers

### Reading Comprehension
Reading comprehension tutoring goes beyond simply understanding what a text says. Tutors teach students how to analyze texts at different levels, identify themes, techniques, and author intent, and develop close reading skills for exams. They also work on building reading stamina and vocabulary, which are essential for tackling longer, more complex texts in high school. Students learn to annotate passages, ask critical questions, and support their interpretations with evidence from the text.

### Writing and Essay Skills
Strong writing is built on clear structure and purposeful argumentation. Tutors teach essay structure and planning, argument development, and academic writing style. They also support creative writing assignments and reinforce grammar and punctuation rules. Many students understand what they want to say but struggle to organize their thoughts on paper. Tutors provide frameworks, feedback on drafts, and practice exercises that help students express themselves clearly and persuasively.

### Exam Preparation
Exam-focused English tutoring prepares students for the specific demands of their qualifications. This includes IGCSE English Language and Literature, IB English A and B, A Level English Literature and Language, and SAT/ACT English sections. Tutors teach students how to decode question prompts, manage time in essay-based exams, and use literary terminology accurately. Past paper practice is a core component of exam preparation.

### English as an Additional Language (EAL)
For students who are not native speakers, EAL tutoring provides targeted support for academic success. Tutors focus on academic vocabulary building, grammar foundations, speaking and listening confidence, and writing fluency. They also provide transition support into English-medium curricula, helping students understand instructions, participate in class discussions, and produce written work that meets international school standards.

## English by Curriculum

Each curriculum approaches English differently, and tutors tailor their teaching to match.

- **IB English** requires deep literary analysis, comparative essays, and oral commentary. English A Literature and Language & Literature students must analyze texts in detail and construct sophisticated arguments. English B students focus on language acquisition and communication skills. Tutors help students meet the specific assessment criteria for each course.
- **IGCSE English** emphasizes both language skills and literary understanding. First Language students work on comprehension, summary writing, and essay technique. Literature students analyze set texts and unseen poetry. Tutors teach mark-scheme strategies so students know what examiners are looking for.
- **A Level English** demands independent critical thinking. Literature students compare texts across genres and periods, while Language students explore linguistics and discourse. Tutors guide students through the transition from IGCSE to A Level, where personal interpretation and originality are rewarded.
- **American Language Arts** integrates reading, writing, speaking, and listening. Tutors support essay writing, literary analysis, and research skills that align with Common Core standards.
- **Cambridge Primary and Lower Secondary English** focus on phonics, reading fluency, and early writing. Tutors use structured activities to build confidence in young learners.

## Common English Challenges

English is a complex subject with many components, and students often struggle in specific areas:

- **Reading comprehension** — Students may read a text but miss deeper meanings, symbolism, or authorial intent. Tutors teach annotation and questioning strategies that unlock layers of meaning.
- **Essay structure** — Many students have good ideas but struggle to organize them into coherent paragraphs. Tutors provide templates, planning techniques, and feedback on drafts.
- **Grammar and syntax** — Small errors in grammar, punctuation, and sentence structure can undermine otherwise strong writing. Tutors diagnose recurring mistakes and teach rules in context.
- **Exam timing** — English exams are often long and essay-heavy. Students run out of time or write too much on one question. Tutors practice timed writing and teach time allocation strategies.
- **EAL transitions** — Non-native speakers may be conversationally fluent but lack academic vocabulary. Tutors bridge the gap between social and academic English, helping students succeed in content-heavy subjects.

## How English Tutoring Progresses Over Time

English tutoring typically unfolds in three phases.

**Phase 1 — Diagnostic and skill building.** The tutor assesses the student's reading level, writing ability, and any gaps in grammar or vocabulary. Early sessions focus on building core skills and establishing good habits.

**Phase 2 — Application and feedback.** The tutor introduces more complex texts, longer essays, and exam-style questions. Students receive detailed feedback on their writing, with specific action points for improvement.

**Phase 3 — Independence and exam readiness.** The tutor steps back, prompting the student to lead analysis and self-edit their work. Revision plans are created for upcoming exams, and the student applies what they have learned with minimal guidance.

This progression is flexible. EAL students may spend longer in Phase 1, while advanced literature students might move quickly to Phase 3.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What age should my child start English tutoring?**
Any age. Younger students often need reading support, while older students need essay and exam help. EAL students may need ongoing support. The best time to start is when you notice a pattern of frustration, declining grades, or teacher feedback suggesting that extra help would be beneficial.

**Which curriculum does your English tutoring follow?**
We follow your child's curriculum, whether IB, IGCSE, A Level, or another system. Our tutors are familiar with the set texts, exam formats, and assessment criteria used by international schools in Thailand.

**Do you offer online English tutoring?**
Yes, online English tutoring works very well, especially for writing feedback and discussion. Tutors can share documents, annotate essays in real time, and conduct oral commentary practice through video calls. Many students find the online format less intimidating and more focused.

**How do I know if my child needs an English tutor?**
Signs include difficulty with writing assignments, low reading comprehension scores, teacher feedback about language skills, or anxiety about English exams. You might also notice that your child avoids reading, takes an unusually long time to complete essays, or expresses frustration with literature analysis.

**Can a tutor help with English exam preparation?**
Yes, exam preparation is a core part of our English tutoring, including past paper practice and essay technique. Tutors teach students how to decode question prompts, plan essays quickly, and use literary terminology accurately. They also provide feedback on timed essays so students understand what to improve.

**Can you help with university application essays?**
Yes. Many high school students need support with personal statements and supplemental essays. Tutors help students identify compelling topics, structure their narratives, and edit for clarity and tone. This support is available for students applying to universities in the UK, US, and other destinations.

**How does EAL tutoring differ from general English tutoring?**
EAL tutoring focuses on the specific language skills needed for academic success in an English-medium school. This includes academic vocabulary, grammar for writing, listening comprehension in classroom settings, and speaking confidence. General English tutoring often focuses on literature and essay skills. Some students need both, and tutors can blend approaches.
