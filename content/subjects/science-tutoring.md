# Science Tutoring in Thailand

Science tutoring covers biology, chemistry, and physics — the three core sciences that students encounter in international curricula. Many students excel in one science but struggle with another, and tutoring can help balance skills and build confidence across all three.

Parents often tell us:

- "My child understands biology but struggles with chemistry"
- "The physics concepts are abstract and hard to visualize"
- "Lab reports take too long and don't get good marks"
- "There's too much content to revise for science exams"
- "My child needs help connecting theory to real-world examples"

## What Science Tutoring Covers

### Biology
Biology tutoring covers cell biology, genetics, and ecology, as well as human physiology, plant biology, and evolution and biodiversity. Tutors follow the specific IB, IGCSE, and A Level biology syllabi, ensuring that students learn the correct terminology and concepts for their exam board. Many students find biology content-heavy, so tutors also teach memorization techniques and how to structure revision notes effectively.

### Chemistry
Chemistry tutoring addresses atomic structure and bonding, organic chemistry, chemical reactions and energetics, stoichiometry and calculations, and lab technique and safety. Chemistry is often the science that students find most abstract, particularly when dealing with sub-microscopic models and mathematical calculations. Tutors use diagrams, analogies, and step-by-step problem solving to make these concepts accessible.

### Physics
Physics tutoring covers mechanics and forces, electricity and magnetism, waves and optics, nuclear and particle physics, and problem-solving and calculations. Physics demands both conceptual understanding and mathematical fluency. Tutors help students set up equations, draw clear diagrams, and apply formulas correctly. They also teach students how to break down multi-step problems into manageable parts.

### General Science
General science tutoring supports combined science courses at the middle school or IGCSE level. Topics include the scientific method and inquiry, data analysis and graphing, and lab report writing. Tutors emphasize how to design fair tests, process results, and draw valid conclusions. These skills are essential for success in all three sciences at the high school level.

## Science by Curriculum

Science is taught differently across curricula, and our tutors adjust their approach accordingly.

- **IB Science** requires students to master detailed content while also developing experimental skills. Biology, Chemistry, and Physics at Standard Level and Higher Level each have their own Internal Assessment. Tutors help students design investigations, process data, and write lab reports that meet IB criteria.
- **IGCSE Science** offers Combined Science, Coordinated Science, and individual sciences. Tutors teach the specific content for each route and focus heavily on exam technique. Students learn how to answer structured questions, interpret data, and perform calculations under timed conditions.
- **A Level Science** dives deep into each discipline. Tutors support the transition from IGCSE by introducing more advanced mathematics, abstract theory, and independent practical work. Many A Level students need help with the synoptic nature of final exams, where topics from across the course are tested together.
- **Cambridge Primary and Lower Secondary Science** introduces scientific inquiry through hands-on activities. Tutors make learning engaging while building the foundational skills that prepare students for IGCSE.

## Common Science Challenges

Students often approach science tutoring with specific frustrations:

- **Too much content** — Science syllabi are packed with facts, equations, and processes. Tutors help students prioritize topics, create concise notes, and use active recall techniques.
- **Abstract concepts** — Ideas like atomic structure, chemical bonding, and electric fields are not directly observable. Tutors use models, simulations, and analogies to make the invisible visible.
- **Mathematical weakness** — Physics and chemistry rely on math skills that some students have not fully mastered. Tutors review the underlying math before applying it to scientific contexts.
- **Lab report writing** — Many students collect good data but lose marks on analysis and evaluation. Tutors teach the structure of a strong lab report and how to discuss errors and limitations.
- **Exam technique** — Science exams often require specific wording, labeled diagrams, and structured explanations. Tutors practice past paper questions and teach students how to read mark schemes.

## How Science Tutoring Progresses Over Time

Science tutoring usually follows a structured path that builds both knowledge and skills.

**Stage 1 — Conceptual foundation.** The tutor reviews core topics, identifies gaps, and ensures the student understands fundamental principles. This stage often involves visual explanations, demonstrations, and guided practice.

**Stage 2 — Application and problem solving.** The tutor introduces exam-style questions, lab scenarios, and multi-step calculations. Students learn to apply their knowledge to unfamiliar contexts, which is a key requirement at the IGCSE and IB levels.

**Stage 3 — Revision and exam readiness.** The tutor creates a personalized revision plan, focusing on weak topics and high-yield areas. Past paper practice intensifies, and the tutor refines exam technique, time management, and confidence.

Students typically move through these stages over one to two terms, depending on their starting point and session frequency.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What age should my child start science tutoring?**
Most science tutoring begins around age 11-12 when students start separate sciences, though some need support earlier in combined science courses. Younger students may benefit from general science tutoring that builds curiosity and foundational skills. The right time depends on when your child begins to struggle or when a challenging curriculum phase approaches.

**Which curriculum does your science tutoring follow?**
We follow your child's curriculum, covering the specific syllabus for IB, IGCSE, A Level, or another system. Our tutors know the exact topics, practical requirements, and exam formats for each board. They also stay updated on syllabus changes that affect what students need to learn.

**Do you offer online science tutoring?**
Yes, online science tutoring works well with digital whiteboards for diagrams and calculations. Tutors can share slides, annotate equations, and walk students through experiments conceptually. For practical-focused topics, tutors explain the theory behind lab work and review lab report drafts.

**How do I know if my child needs a science tutor?**
Signs include difficulty with science homework, low test scores, anxiety about lab work, or teacher feedback about understanding gaps. You might also notice that your child memorizes facts but cannot apply them to new situations, or that they avoid science revision in favor of other subjects.

**Can a tutor help with science exam preparation?**
Yes, exam-focused science tutoring is one of our most requested services, especially for IGCSE and IB. Tutors use past papers to familiarize students with question types, teach mark-scheme strategies, and review the topics that appear most frequently. They also help students manage the volume of content without becoming overwhelmed.

**Can a tutor help with IB science Internal Assessments?**
Yes. The IB Internal Assessment is a significant portion of the final grade. Tutors help students choose appropriate research questions, design valid experiments, process data accurately, and write clear evaluations. They also review drafts to ensure the work meets IB assessment criteria.

**Do you tutor all three sciences, or just one?**
We tutor biology, chemistry, and physics individually, as well as combined or coordinated science courses. Some students need support in all three sciences, while others need help with just one weak area. We match tutors based on the specific subjects your child needs.
