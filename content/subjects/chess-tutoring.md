## Frequently Asked Questions

**What age should my child start chess tutoring?**
Most chess tutoring begins around age 6-7, when children can concentrate for longer periods and understand abstract concepts. However, keen younger children can start with simplified instruction.

**Which curriculum does your chess tutoring follow?**
Chess tutoring follows a structured skill progression rather than a school curriculum. We adapt to the student's level, whether beginner, intermediate, or advanced.

**Do you offer online chess tutoring?**
Yes, online chess tutoring works well using chess platforms that allow real-time play and analysis together.

**How do I know if my child needs a chess tutor?**
Signs include a genuine interest in chess, participation in school chess clubs, preparation for competitions, or a desire to develop strategic thinking skills.

**Can a tutor help with chess competition preparation?**
Yes, tutors can help students prepare for tournaments by building opening knowledge, reviewing games, and practicing competitive play.

**How long are chess tutoring sessions?**
Sessions are typically one hour. Competition-focused sessions may include longer practice games.

**What should my child bring to a chess tutoring session?**
A chess set and board, any game scores from previous matches, and a notebook for recording openings and key ideas.
