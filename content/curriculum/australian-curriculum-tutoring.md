## What Is the Australian Curriculum?

The Australian Curriculum is organized as follows:

- **Foundation to Year 2** — Ages 5-7. Focus on literacy, numeracy, and social development.
- **Years 3-6** — Ages 8-11. Broadening into Science, Humanities, and the Arts.
- **Years 7-10** — Ages 12-15. Increased subject depth with preparation for senior secondary study.
- **Senior Years** — Some schools offer Australian senior qualifications such as HSC (Higher School Certificate) or VCE (Victorian Certificate of Education).

Key features:

- **National framework** — Developed by the Australian Curriculum, Assessment and Reporting Authority (ACARA)
- **Eight learning areas** — English, Mathematics, Science, Humanities and Social Sciences, The Arts, Technologies, Health and Physical Education, and Languages
- **General capabilities** — Emphasis on literacy, numeracy, critical thinking, and digital literacy
- **Cross-curriculum priorities** — Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander histories, sustainability, and engagement with Asia
