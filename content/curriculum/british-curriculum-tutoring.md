## What Is the British Curriculum?

The British National Curriculum is organized into Key Stages:

- **Key Stage 1** — Ages 5-7 (Years 1-2). Focus on reading, writing, and basic mathematics.
- **Key Stage 2** — Ages 7-11 (Years 3-6). Broader subjects including science, history, and geography. SATs at the end of Year 6.
- **Key Stage 3** — Ages 11-14 (Years 7-9). Foundation for GCSE with increased subject depth.
- **Key Stage 4** — Ages 14-16 (Years 10-11). GCSE or IGCSE qualifications. Students typically take 8-10 subjects.
- **Sixth Form** — Ages 16-18 (Years 12-13). A Level qualifications in 3-4 subjects.

Key features:

- **National standards** — The curriculum is set by the UK government, though international schools may adapt it
- **External exams** — GCSE/IGCSE and A Level exams are set by UK exam boards
- **University pathway** — A Levels are the standard qualification for UK university entry
- **Subject breadth then depth** — Students study a wide range of subjects before specializing
