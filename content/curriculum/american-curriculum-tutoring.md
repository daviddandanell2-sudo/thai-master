## What Is the American Curriculum?

The American curriculum is typically structured as follows:

- **Elementary School** — Grades K-5 (ages 5-10). Core subjects include English, Math, Science, and Social Studies.
- **Middle School** — Grades 6-8 (ages 11-13). Increased subject depth with introduction to foreign languages and electives.
- **High School** — Grades 9-12 (ages 14-18). Students earn credits toward graduation. Many schools offer Advanced Placement (AP) courses.

Key features:

- **Common Core or school-specific standards** — Some schools follow Common Core State Standards; others use their own frameworks
- **Credit-based graduation** — Students accumulate credits across subjects to earn a high school diploma
- **Grade Point Average (GPA)** — Continuous assessment contributes to an overall GPA used for university applications
- **Standardized tests** — SAT or ACT scores are typically required for US university admissions
- **AP courses** — College-level courses that can earn university credit
