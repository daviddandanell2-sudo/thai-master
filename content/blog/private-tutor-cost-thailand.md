# How Much Does a Private Tutor Cost in Thailand? A Parent's Guide

If you're considering hiring a private tutor for your child in Thailand, one of the first questions you'll ask is: how much will this cost? The answer depends on several factors — the tutor's experience, your child's curriculum, the subject, and where you live. This guide breaks down what you can expect to pay and what drives those costs.

## The Short Answer: What Tutoring Costs in Thailand

Based on market research across tutoring platforms, agencies, and independent tutors in Thailand, here's what parents typically pay:

| Provider Type | Rate (THB/hour) | Rate (USD/hour) | What You Typically Get |
|--------------|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Budget online platforms** | 260 – 1,000 | $8 – 30 | General subject help, variable tutor quality, limited curriculum expertise |
| **Marketplace sites** | 500 – 2,000 | $15 – 60 | Wide tutor pool, self-selection, quality varies significantly |
| **Mid-range tutors / agencies** | 1,500 – 2,500 | $45 – 75 | Vetted tutors, curriculum-specific expertise, structured support |
| **Premium agencies** | 2,500 – 4,000+ | $75 – 120+ | Examiner-led tutoring, highly qualified tutors, premium positioning |

These are broad ranges. Within each category, pricing can vary based on the factors below.

## What Affects Tutoring Costs

Not all tutors can teach all curricula. A tutor who knows the IB Mathematics syllabus inside out will typically charge more than a general math tutor because their expertise is rarer and more valuable.

### 2. Subject Difficulty

Some subjects command higher rates due to the specialized knowledge required:

- **Math and sciences** (physics, chemistry, biology) — typically mid-to-premium rates
- **English language and literature** — wide range, from budget conversation practice to premium literature analysis
- **Humanities** (history, geography, economics) — typically mid-range
- **Languages** (Thai, Mandarin, French, Spanish) — wide range depending on tutor background

## Hidden Costs to Watch For

When comparing tutoring options, look beyond the hourly rate:

### Registration Fees
Some agencies charge a one-time registration fee of 1,000 – 5,000 THB before you even meet a tutor. Ask upfront.

### Package Lock-Ins
Some services require you to buy 10, 20, or 50 hours upfront. While this may reduce the hourly rate, it locks you in before you know if the tutor is a good fit.

### Travel Charges
For in-person tutoring outside central areas, some tutors add travel costs. Clarify whether the quoted rate includes travel.

### Materials Fees
Some tutors charge extra for past papers, worksheets, or proprietary materials. Others include these in the hourly rate.

### Cancellation Policies
Check the cancellation policy. Some tutors charge full price for cancellations with less than 24 hours' notice.

## Online vs. In-Person: Does It Affect Cost?

Online tutoring is often slightly cheaper than in-person because the tutor doesn't need to travel. However, the difference is usually smaller than parents expect — typically 10-20% less, not 50%. The tutor's expertise and time are the main cost drivers, not travel.

For families in locations with limited local tutor availability (Hua Hin, Koh Samui, smaller cities), online tutoring is often the only way to access curriculum specialists. In these cases, the value isn't in the price reduction — it's in access to expertise that simply isn't available locally.

## How to Get Value for Money

### Start with a Trial Session
Never commit to a long-term package before meeting the tutor. A single trial session lets you assess:
- Does the tutor know the curriculum?
- Does your child respond well to their teaching style?
- Is the tutor punctual and professional?

### Be Clear About Goals
Tutors can work more efficiently when they know what you want. Instead of "help with math," say "prepare for IGCSE Extended Mathematics exams in May, focusing on algebra and trigonometry."

### Consider Frequency
One intensive session per week with a specialist tutor often delivers better results than two sessions with a less experienced tutor. Quality usually beats quantity.

### Ask About Progress Reporting
A tutor who provides regular updates on your child's progress adds value beyond the session itself. You know what's working and what needs adjustment.

## The Bottom Line

Tutoring in Thailand ranges from 260 THB per hour for budget online platforms to 4,000+ THB per hour for premium examiner-led services. For most families with children in international schools, a reasonable expectation is 1,500 – 2,500 THB per hour for a qualified tutor with curriculum-specific expertise.

The cheapest option isn't always the best value. A tutor who doesn't know the IB syllabus may cost less per hour but waste sessions re-teaching content your child already knows, or teaching it in a way that doesn't align with IB assessment criteria.

The most expensive option isn't always necessary either. A premium examiner-led tutor may be overkill for a Year 9 student who just needs help keeping up with classwork.

The right tutor is the one who matches your child's needs, curriculum, and goals at a price that fits your budget.
