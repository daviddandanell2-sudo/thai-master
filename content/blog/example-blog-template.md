# Example Blog Article Title

## Hook

Start with a relatable scenario or question that grabs the reader's attention. This should immediately confirm that the article is for them.

> "You've just moved to Bangkok. Your child is starting at a new international school, and you're trying to understand whether the IB or IGCSE curriculum is the better choice. Every parent you meet has a different opinion. You need clear, unbiased information to make the right decision for your family."

## The Problem

Explain the problem in detail. Why is this topic important? What do parents typically struggle with?

Moving to a new country with school-age children brings a unique set of challenges. Beyond finding a home and settling in, you need to navigate an unfamiliar education system. International schools offer different curricula, each with its own strengths, and the choice you make can affect your child's university options and academic experience.

Many parents feel overwhelmed by the acronyms — IB, IGCSE, A Level, AP — and the conflicting advice they receive. Some schools push one curriculum over another. Other parents swear by their own experience. But what matters is what's right for your child.

## The Solution or Research

Provide the main content. This is the meat of the article. Use subheadings to break up long sections.

### Understanding the Options

**IB (International Baccalaureate)** is an inquiry-based program that emphasizes critical thinking, research skills, and global awareness. It suits students who are intellectually curious and self-motivated.

**IGCSE (International General Certificate of Secondary Education)** is a British qualification taken at age 14-16. It provides a solid foundation in core subjects and is excellent preparation for A Levels.

**A Level (Advanced Level)** is a two-year program for students aged 16-18. It allows deep specialization in 3-4 subjects and is the standard pathway to UK universities.

### Key Differences

| Factor | IB | IGCSE | A Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Age range | 3-19 | 14-16 | 16-18 |
| Number of subjects | 6 in DP | 8-10 | 3-4 |
| Assessment | Exams + coursework | Mostly exams | Mostly exams |
| University focus | Global | UK/Commonwealth | UK/Commonwealth |

## Practical Advice

Give the reader something they can do today.

### Steps to Choose the Right Curriculum

1. **Consider your child's strengths** — Do they prefer breadth or depth? Are they self-motivated or do they need structure?
2. **Think about university goals** — Where do they want to study? Some curricula are better recognized in certain countries.
3. **Visit schools** — Talk to teachers and students. See how the curriculum is actually taught.
4. **Talk to a tutor** — A tutor who knows multiple curricula can give you unbiased advice based on your child's needs.
