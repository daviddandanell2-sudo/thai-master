## Sections

### 1. Hero Headline (H1)

**Pattern:** "{Subject Name} Tutoring in Thailand"

**Example:** "Math Tutoring in Thailand"

**Rules:**
- Include primary keyword
- Keep under 60 characters

### 2. Parent Problem Statement

**Purpose:** Acknowledge why parents seek tutoring for this subject.

**Pattern:**
> [Subject] can be one of the most challenging subjects for students in international schools. Whether your child is struggling with [specific topic], preparing for [exam], or needs to build confidence, the right tutor makes a significant difference.

**Rules:**
- 2-4 sentences
- Mention specific challenges for this subject

### 3. What {Subject} Tutoring Covers

**Purpose:** Explain what the tutoring includes.

**Required elements:**
- Topics covered at different levels
- How tutoring adapts to different ages
- Connection to curricula (IB, IGCSE, A Level, etc.)

**Example for Math:**
> Our math tutoring covers:
> - Primary math and numeracy
> - Middle school math and pre-algebra
> - IGCSE Mathematics (Core and Extended)
> - IB Math (Analysis and Approaches, Applications and Interpretation)
> - A Level Mathematics and Further Mathematics
> - Exam technique and problem-solving strategies

**Rules:**
- 150-300 words
- Specific, not vague
- Link to related curriculum pages

### 4. Why Parents Worry About {Subject}

**Purpose:** Show empathy and understanding.

**Pattern:**
> Parents often tell us:
> - "My child understands the concepts but makes careless mistakes"
> - "The curriculum has moved too fast and my child has gaps"
> - "Exam questions are much harder than homework"
> - "My child lacks confidence in [subject]"

**Rules:**
- 100-200 words
- Real parent concerns
- Show that tutoring addresses these

### 5. Curricula That Include {Subject}
