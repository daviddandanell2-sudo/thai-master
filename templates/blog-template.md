## Sections

### 1. Headline (H1)

**Pattern:** Clear, specific promise.

**Examples:**
- "How to Find a Private Tutor in Bangkok: A Parent's Guide"
- "IGCSE vs A Level: Which Is Right for Your Child?"
- "Moving to Phuket with Children: School and Tutoring Options"

**Rules:**
- Include primary keyword near the beginning
- Make a clear promise
- Keep under 70 characters

### 2. Hook (First Paragraph)

**Purpose:** Grab attention and confirm this article is for the reader.

**Pattern:**
> [Relatable scenario or question that the parent has experienced]
> [Brief hint that this article has the answer]

**Example:**
> "You've just moved to Bangkok. Your child is settling into a new international school, but the math curriculum is more advanced than you expected. You're wondering if a tutor could help — but where do you start?"

**Rules:**
- 2-4 sentences
- Immediately relatable
- No fluff

### 3. Problem (H2)

**Purpose:** Define the problem clearly.

**Pattern:**
> ## The Challenge of [Topic]
>
> [Explain the problem in detail. Why is this hard? What do parents typically struggle with?]

**Rules:**
- 100-200 words
- Show understanding of parent pain points
- Use "you" to speak directly to the reader

### 4. Research or Solution (H2)

**Purpose:** Provide the answer or insight.

**Pattern:**
> ## [Solution or Answer Headline]
>
> [Main content. This is the meat of the article.]
> - [Key point 1]
> - [Key point 2]
> - [Key point 3]

**Rules:**
- 400-800 words
- Be specific and actionable
- Use subheadings (H3) to break up long sections
- Include examples where possible

### 5. Practical Advice (H2)

**Purpose:** Give the reader something they can do today.

**Pattern:**
> ## Practical Steps for [Topic]
>
> 1. [Actionable step 1]
> 2. [Actionable step 2]
> 3. [Actionable step 3]

**Rules:**
- 200-400 words
- Numbered lists work well
- Each step should be concrete

### 6. Case Study or Example (Optional H2)

**Purpose:** Make it real.

**Pattern:**
> ## Example: [Brief scenario]
>
> [A real-ish scenario without fake names or fake results]
> "A family moving to Chiang Mai found that their child was behind in science compared to the new school's curriculum. With six weeks of focused tutoring, the child caught up and felt confident starting the new term."

**Rules:**
- No fake testimonials
- No specific names unless real and approved
- Keep it general but relatable

### 7. Internal Links

**Purpose:** Connect to service pages.

**Pattern:**
> **Related:** If you're looking for tutoring support, see our guides to:
> - [Tutoring in relevant location]
> - [Relevant subject tutoring]
> - [Relevant curriculum tutoring]

**Rules:**
- At least 1 service page link
- Contextual, not forced
- Descriptive anchor text

### 8. Call to Action (Final Paragraph)

**Purpose:** Convert readers into inquiries.

**Pattern:**
> Need personalized help? Our tutors work with families across Thailand. [Message us on WhatsApp](#) to discuss your child's needs.

**Rules:**
- Soft, not aggressive
- Related to the article topic
- One clear action

## Writing Rules for Blog Articles

- **Word count:** 1200-2500 words
- **Paragraphs:** 2-4 sentences max
- **Tone:** Helpful, informative, not salesy
- **Facts:** Verify before publishing. No invented statistics.
- **Links:** Link to service pages where relevant
- **CTA:** Every article ends with a soft CTA
- **SEO:** Primary keyword in H1, first paragraph, and at least one H2
